Momo Recipes

The dish is believed to be of Tibetan origin and since then
has spread to
neighbouring countries,along with the influx of the tibetan diaspora.

Originally,the filling of the dish was typicallymeat,such as YAK,due to scarcity
of vegetables in Tibet.

However, after arriving in other neighbouring countries, the momo was made vegetarian
in the modern era to feed the large population of vegatraian.

v > Ingredients

  • white Flour
  • Minced Meat
  • onions/shallots
  • garlic
  • soysauce
  • salt
  • msg (optional)
Preparation
  1. A little yeast or baking soda is added to give a more doughty texture to finished prodcut.
  2. The dough is rolled into small circular flat pieces. The filling is then enclosed in the circular dough cover either in a round pocket or in a half-moon or crescent shape.
  3. A little oil is sometimes added to the lean ground/minced meat to keep the filling moist and juicy.
  4. The dumplings are then cooked by steaming over a soup (either a stock based on bones or vegetables) in a momo-making utensil called mucktoo.
  5. Those who dont have steamer then also be pan fried or deep-fried after being steamed or just wihtout steaming.
Cooking Time

00:15 mins to 00:20 depend on thickness of your dough.

How to serve

Momo is usually served with a dipping sauce ,which is made of red chilly,
black pepper and tomato paste.

Or just dip into soysauce or any dipping sauce that you like.

we usually served with hot bone broth or hot Yak butter milk tea.
